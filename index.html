Public Opinion on China (2019-2024)

The attitudes of the Kazakh people towards China are an intricate mixture of favorable and critical views, often rooted in cultural differences and political developments.

Surveys taken recently suggest an incremental improvement in public opinion on China. A survey conducted by the TALAP Center for Applied Research reported that over 70% of Kazakhstanis have a generally favorable view of China. Approximately 80% of respondents support developing economic relations with China.

However, these positive trends are tempered by concerns about China's treatment of ethnic Kazakhs and Uyghurs in Xinjiang, which has fueled some ambivalence.

Migration Policy Analysis (2019-2024) and Applicability for Kazakhstan

The United States has a rich history of integrating immigrants through various programs, ranging from visa policies to support for ethnic communities. Key elements that helped the U.S. successfully integrate migrants include:

  • Support programs for migrants: The U.S. provides educational, labor, and economic programs that help migrants adapt to their new lives.
  • Integration platforms: Various online platforms offer information on legal aid, integration courses, and even create social communities for migrants.
  • Social mobility and economic participation: Migrants are actively involved in small and medium-sized businesses, which contributes to economic growth.

From 2019 to 2024, U.S. migration policy saw significant changes, particularly in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic and political developments:

  • 2019-2020: Entry restrictions driven by Trump administration policies and the pandemic reduced migration flows.
  • 2021-2024: A change in administration led to the easing of visa and migration restrictions and an expansion of integration programs.

Benefits:

  • Increased opportunities for migrants to create businesses, contributing to economic growth.
  • Strengthening the role of educational and language programs for the successful integration of migrants.

Drawbacks:

  • Frequent changes in visa policy can destabilize migration flows.
  • Some groups of migrants face challenges adapting due to insufficient social support.

Applicability for Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan can use the U.S. experience in creating platforms for the cultural integration and economic participation of migrants. For example:

  • Educational programs for migrants: Kazakhstan can offer retraining and education courses to improve the social and economic skills of migrants, similar to the U.S.
  • Online platforms and social networks: Developing a platform for communication, experience exchange, and job searching could significantly accelerate the integration of migrants into Kazakh society.
